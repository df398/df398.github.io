About me

I’m a Herchel Smith Research Fellow at Cambridge University in the Chemistry Department. My research interests include Computational Molecular Science, Artificial Intelligence and Statistical Mechanics. I develop and use theory, numerical algorithms and simulation to study reactive energy landscapes and dynamics of complex systems.

I received my PhD in Theoretical Chemistry from the Fritz Haber Research Centre in HUJI where I worked with Ronnie Kosloff and Yehuda Zeiri on computational studies of condensed-phase reactive systems from at a molecular level.

Previous collaborators include Bill Goddard, Adri van Duin and Sergey Zybin. Currently, I work closely with David Wales FRS.
